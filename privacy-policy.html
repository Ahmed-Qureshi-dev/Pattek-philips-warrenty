Patek Philippe SA ("Patek Philippe", "we", "our", or "us"), headquartered at Chemin du Pont-du-Centenaire 141, 1228 Plan-les-Ouates, Geneva, Switzerland, is committed to protecting the privacy of all individuals who interact with our products, services, and platforms. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your personal data in connection with our warranty registration, after-sales service, and related activities.
01

Information We Collect

When you register your Patek Philippe timepiece or interact with our after-sales services, we may collect the following categories of personal information:

  • Full name, postal address, email address, and telephone number
  • Timepiece details including reference number, serial number, and model designation
  • Purchase date, purchase price, and authorized dealer information
  • Service history and repair records associated with your timepiece
  • Communication preferences and language settings
  • Device and browsing data when you access our digital platforms

We collect this information directly from you at the time of warranty registration, through our authorized dealers, or via our official website and mobile applications.

02

How We Use Your Data

Your personal data is used exclusively for the following purposes, always in accordance with applicable data protection legislation:

  • Administering and validating your international warranty coverage
  • Providing after-sales service, maintenance, and technical support
  • Authenticating the provenance and ownership of your timepiece
  • Communicating with you regarding service updates, recalls, or important notices
  • Improving our products and services through aggregated, anonymised analysis
  • Complying with our legal and regulatory obligations

We process your data on the legal bases of contractual necessity (to fulfil our warranty obligations), legitimate interests, and, where required, your explicit consent.

03

Data Sharing & Disclosure

Patek Philippe does not sell, rent, or trade your personal information to any third parties. Your data may be shared only in the following limited circumstances:

  • With authorised Patek Philippe retailers and service centres for warranty and repair purposes
  • With carefully vetted technology service providers acting as data processors on our behalf
  • With regulatory authorities or courts where required by applicable law
  • With successor entities in the event of a corporate restructuring, subject to equivalent privacy protections
All third parties with whom we share data are bound by strict contractual obligations to process your information solely as instructed by Patek Philippe and to maintain equivalent standards of confidentiality and security.
04

Data Retention

We retain your personal data for as long as is necessary to fulfil the purposes outlined in this policy, or as required by applicable law. Specifically:

  • Warranty and ownership records are retained for the duration of your timepiece's serviceable life plus an additional period to meet legal obligations
  • Service and repair records are maintained for a minimum of ten (10) years from the date of service
  • Marketing preferences are retained until you withdraw your consent

Upon expiry of the applicable retention period, your data will be securely deleted or irreversibly anonymised.

05

Your Rights

Subject to applicable data protection law, you are entitled to exercise the following rights in relation to your personal data:

  • Right of Access — to obtain a copy of the personal data we hold about you
  • Right to Rectification — to have inaccurate or incomplete data corrected
  • Right to Erasure — to request deletion of your data where no legitimate retention basis applies
  • Right to Restriction — to limit how we process your data in certain circumstances
  • Right to Portability — to receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Right to Object — to object to processing based on legitimate interests or direct marketing
  • Right to Withdraw Consent — to withdraw consent at any time without affecting prior lawful processing

To exercise any of these rights, please contact our Data Protection Officer using the details provided in the Contact section below. We will respond to all verified requests within thirty (30) days.

06

Security Measures

Patek Philippe employs industry-leading technical and organisational measures to safeguard your personal data against unauthorised access, alteration, disclosure, or destruction. Our security framework includes:

  • End-to-end encryption for all data transmitted via our digital platforms
  • Role-based access controls limiting internal data access to authorised personnel only
  • Regular security audits, vulnerability assessments, and penetration testing
  • Staff training on data protection obligations and secure handling practices
  • Incident response protocols meeting Swiss and EU regulatory requirements

In the event of a data breach that poses a risk to your rights, we will notify you and the relevant supervisory authority without undue delay, as required by law.

07

International Transfers

As a globally operating Swiss Maison, Patek Philippe may transfer your personal data to authorised service partners located outside your country of residence. Where such transfers occur, we ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place, including:

  • Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved by the European Commission
  • Binding Corporate Rules for intra-group transfers
  • Adequacy decisions recognising equivalent protection in the destination country

All international transfers are conducted in full compliance with Swiss Federal Act on Data Protection (FADP) and, where applicable, the European General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR).

08

Cookies & Tracking

Our digital platforms use cookies and similar tracking technologies to ensure optimal functionality and to enhance your experience. Cookies are categorised as follows:

  • Strictly Necessary — essential for core platform functionality and cannot be disabled
  • Performance — used to analyse aggregated usage patterns and improve our services
  • Preference — used to remember your settings and personalisation choices
  • Marketing — used only with your explicit prior consent for relevant communications

You may manage your cookie preferences at any time through our Cookie Preference Centre, accessible on any page of our website. Withdrawing consent for non-essential cookies will not affect your use of our core services.

09

Children's Privacy

Our services and products are not directed to individuals under the age of eighteen (18). We do not knowingly collect personal data from minors. If we become aware that we have inadvertently received personal information from a person under the age of eighteen, we will take immediate steps to delete such information from our records. If you believe a minor has provided us with personal data, please contact us immediately.

10

Changes to This Policy

Patek Philippe reserves the right to update this Privacy Policy periodically to reflect changes in our practices, legal requirements, or service offerings. When material changes are made, we will notify you by email (where we hold your contact details) or via a prominent notice on our website, prior to the change becoming effective.

We encourage you to review this policy periodically. Continued use of our services following notification of changes constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.

The most current version of this Privacy Policy is always available at patek.com/privacy-policy. The version number and effective date are indicated at the top of this document.
